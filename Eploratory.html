EXPLANATORY DATA ANALYSIS OF LOAN ISSUANCE AND DEFAULT PATTERNS IN FINANCIAL INSTITUTION

BUSINESS CONTEXT

My client is a financial institution that specializes in consumer loans. Their portfolio includes diverse loan products such as personal loans, education loans, and business venture loans, medical loans and debt-consolidation to a wide range of customer demographics.

What kind of data do they have?

The datatset provided by the client contains information about customer loans, including customer demographics, loan details, and default status. It is a Loan Default Prediction Dataset. This data is well structured.

Why do they need you as a data scientist?

The organization needs me to help them in predicting loan default risk, by understanding customer profiles and loan characteristics that influence default risks, help them in strategic decision making by refining their credit policies, lending criteria, and marketing strategies.

My intervebntion as a data scientist will enable them to use their data to answer strategic business questions, showcase actionable insights, and enhance their financial operations.

Data Source

The data used in this analysis is from Kaggle, a public source and here is the link to my data source;

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/prakashraushan/loan-dataset

Business Questions

  1. Which age groups and income brackets should we prioritize for loan approvals in order to minimize default rates and hence improve business?

  2. The business intends to conduct direct marketing by visiting different organizations and target specific employees to sell the loans to. Which category of employees should our sales-persons focus in in terms of the above features(Age and Income) in order to attract clients who are less likely to default?

  3. In order to maximize profits, we want to find the best balance between interest rates and loan default, avoiding defaulters. Which loan grades offer that?

  4. As we make future lending decisions, which loan purposes should be prioritized because of the lower likelihood of default?

  5. Given the diverse nature of customers, should we consider employment duration when lending to our customers in the future? Why is that so?

In[10]:
#Filling the missing values in the dataset
data['customer_id'] = data['customer_id'].interpolate()
data['employment_duration'] = data['employment_duration'].fillna(data['employment_duration'].mean())
data['loan_amnt'] = data['loan_amnt'].fillna(data['loan_amnt'].mean())
data['loan_int_rate'] = data['loan_int_rate'].fillna(data['loan_int_rate'].mean())
data['historical_default'] = data['historical_default'].fillna(data['historical_default'].mode()[0])
data['Current_loan_status'] = data['Current_loan_status'].fillna(data['Current_loan_status'].mode()[0])

Various techniques have been used to resolve the NaN values.

For customer ID, we opted for interpolate since the values are Numeric and Sequential. The other categorical columns employed mean, since the mean value basically carries the weight of the dataset. Replacing the missing values in employment_duration and loan_amt with the mean ensures that the distribution remains close to the original.

Historical_default and Current_loan_status used mode since they are categorical columns. There are only 4 missing values for Current_loan_status, therefore not a very high impact. We will avoid using historical_default in our analysis due to large number of missing values.

Final Discussion and Conclusion

Strengths and Limitations of the Explanatory Data Analysis (EDA)

Strengths:

  1. The analysis provided is evidence-based or data driven.It answers the business questions using statistical methods and visualizations, enabling more informed decision-making.
  2. The use of histograms and other visualization techniques allowed for an intuitive understanding of key patterns and trends.
  3. The insights relevant in adressing the financial institutions concerns, such as loan approvals, default risk, and target customers etc

Limitations:

  1. The dataset may not be fully representative of all future borrowers, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
  2. The analysis did not incorporate external economic conditions, regulatory changes, or industry trends that may impact loan defaults.

Insights and Implications for the Business

  1. Question 5

    • Customers with longer employment durations generally exhibited lower default rates. This suggests that employment stability should be considered in loan approval decisions to reduce risk.
    • However, excluding customers with shorter employment durations may limit market reach, requiring a balanced approach.

  2. Question 3:

    • The institution should focus on promoting loans within the B-C range to maintain profitability while minimizing default risks.

  3. Question 2

    • Employees in higher-income brackets and older age groups were less likely to default.
    • Sales efforts should target professionals in stable industries and employees with consistent incomes to enhance loan repayment rates.

  4. Question 1

    • Middle-aged individuals (30-50 years) with stable incomes had lower default rates, making them an ideal target group for loan approvals.
    • Younger customers (below 30) had higher default risks, suggesting a need for stricter eligibility criteria or financial literacy programs for this group.

  5. Question 4

    • Loans for debt consolidation and home improvement had relatively low default rates, making them safer investment options.
    • Loans for discretionary spending, such as vacations, showed higher default risks and should be carefully assessed before approval.

Data-Driven Recommendations

  1. The organization should Incorporate employment duration as a key factor but allow flexibility for high-income earners with shorter work histories.
  2. Focusiong on mid-tier loan grades (B and C) will optimize profitability while maintaining manageable default rates.
  3. Direct marketing efforts toward employees in stable professions and higher-income brackets.
  4. Prioritize safer loan purposes such as debt consolidation and home improvement to reduce default risk.

The financial institution can enhance lending decisions, maximize profitability, and minimize risk, leading to a stronger and more sustainable business model if they follow the provided insights above
